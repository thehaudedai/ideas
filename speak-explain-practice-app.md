# Speaking/Explanation Practice App

## Core Idea

An app to **practice speaking and explaining**. Each day you get a **random Wikipedia article** (or short text) to read for ~2 minutes.

After reading:

1.  30 seconds to **mentally recollect** what you read.

2.  4+ minutes of **voice recording** where you explain the article as if teaching someone.

3.  Rate yourself on how well you explained it.

Repeat **daily**, and after X days, you **listen to old recordings and rate again** to see progress over time.

Basically: **read → recall → explain → rate → repeat → track progress**.

---

## Problem

-   Many people **struggle to explain ideas clearly**.
-   Short ideas are hard to expand into full explanations.
-   Hard to notice gradual improvement in speaking and idea articulation.
-   Self-practice is unstructured and progress isn't tracked over time.

---

## Solution

-   Daily structured exercise: **read + recollect + explain + rate**.
-   Uses **random articles** to give variety and prevent repetition.
-   Tracking old recordings and **re-rating after days** shows progress clearly.
-   Helps users **practice clarity, memory, and expanding ideas verbally**.

---

## How It Helps

-   Builds **confidence in speaking and explaining**.
-   Improves **ability to elongate short ideas into full explanations**.
-   Makes **progress visible** over time through recorded history and ratings.
-   Creates a **habit of deliberate practice** every day.

---

## Extra Thoughts / Features

-   Set time limits for reading, recollection, and recording.
-   Optional **speech playback with transcription** for easier review.
-   Tag or categorize recordings (topics, difficulty).
-   Gamify with streaks or improvement tracking.
-   Could integrate **daily notifications** to make it a habit.
